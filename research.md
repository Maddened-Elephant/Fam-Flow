# Problem Validation Research

## Hypothesis
"Indian families struggle to coordinate schedules, chores, 
and shared expenses because tools are fragmented."

## Research Methodology
- 20 in-depth interviews across:
  - 8 mothers
  - 5 fathers
  - 4 teenagers
  - 3 grandparents
- Urban (12) and semi-urban (8) families
- 2-4 person households with kids aged 5-18

## Key Findings

### SIGNAL 1: Problem is REAL
- 16/20 families mentioned coordination challenges unprompted
- Top pain: "I'm the bottleneck" (mentioned by 14 families)
- Average pain score: 7.2/10

### SIGNAL 2: Problem is FREQUENT
- 18/20 families deal with this daily/weekly
- Average: 2-3 coordination frustrations per week

### SIGNAL 3: Current solutions are broken
- Workarounds: Physical calendars (9), WhatsApp (15), Spreadsheets (3), Nothing (2)
- All solutions are fragmented and manual

### SIGNAL 4: Families want a solution
- 16/20 would try a better app
- 12/20 said they'd pay ₹99-299/month
- 18/20 said it would "save time" (their estimate: 5-10 hours/month)

## Key Quotes
- "I'm tired of being the single point of truth in this house" — Priya, mother of 2
- "We never know when my mom is picking up vs my dad" — Aarya, 10-year-old
- "We fight about money because nobody tracks what's actually being spent" — Rajesh, father of 2
- "I'd pay for something that makes my life easier" — Sunita, 45, mother of 3

## What Surprised Us
- Grandparents are involved in 60% of families (not just parents)
- Kids actually want responsibility if the system is clear
- Money fights are more frequent than chore fights
- Extended family coordination is even messier than nuclear family

## Conclusion
✅ This is a REAL, FREQUENT, PAINFUL problem
✅ Current solutions suck
✅ Families want something better
✅ Willing to pay for it
